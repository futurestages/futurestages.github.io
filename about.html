ABOUT

'A flawless blend of Broadway, games, pop and comedy bliss!' (Fame Magazine). When the hero character of a video game dies, an NPC turns to the audience for help navigating their grief. Part live concert, part escape room, part immersive theatre, each performance is entirely responsive to the player style, improvisation and decisions of the audience. Four audience players join writer-performer Brendan Bradley on stage, wearing virtual reality headsets while the virtual world and characters are projected on the big screen, inviting both theatre and game lovers to a new kind of play.

Live musical theater meets video games as Creator and Performer, Brendan Andolsek Bradley, invites two-tiers of audience to enter and impact the story arc and help a character without agency navigate their grief through interactivity, immersion and song.

MEET THE TEAM

BRENDAN BRADLEY (Writer, Director, Performer) makes his Edinburgh Fringe Festival debut with the world premiere run of NON-PLAYER CHARACTER. Other interactive performance credits include NON-PLAYER CHARACTER (Edinburgh Fringe Festival), DEGENERATE (VR Awards), RESIDENT EVIL:VILLAGE (Capcom), MIDNIGHT SUNS (Marvel), ONBOARDXR, and JETTISON for which he was a Finalist for The Producer Guild of America’s Innovation Award. His film and television work spans over 100 titles, including the upcoming SUCCUBUS (starring opposite Ron Perlman and Rosanna Arquette) THE DAYLONG BROTHERS (starring opposite Keith Carradine). He received his training at The Royal Academy of Dramatic Arts (RADA) in London and New York University’s Tisch School of the Arts where he founded The Brendan Bradley Integrative Technology Lab for introducing real-time storytelling technology into the live arts curriculum. More at brendanAbradley.com @brendanAbradley
